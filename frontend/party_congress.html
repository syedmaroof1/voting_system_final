Indian National Congress

"Haath Badlega Halaat" - The Hand Will Change Circumstances

About the Party

The Indian National Congress (INC), often called the Congress Party, is one of the oldest and most prominent political parties in India. Founded in 1885, it played a pivotal role in India's independence movement and has been a major force in Indian politics since independence in 1947.

Key Principles

  • Secularism: Equal respect for all religions
  • Social Justice: Upliftment of marginalized communities
  • Economic Equality: Reducing income disparities
  • Democratic Values: Strengthening democratic institutions
  • Inclusive Growth: Development for all sections of society

Major Achievements

  • Led India's Independence Movement
  • MGNREGA (Rural Employment Guarantee)
  • Right to Information Act
  • Right to Education Act
  • Food Security Act
  • Green Revolution
  • White Revolution

Vision for Future

The Congress party envisions an India based on the principles of justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity. It aims to create an inclusive society where every citizen has equal opportunities for growth and development, with special focus on social welfare and economic justice.
