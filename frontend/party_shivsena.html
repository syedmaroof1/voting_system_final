Shiv Sena

"Jai Maharashtra" - Victory to Maharashtra

About the Party

Shiv Sena is a regional political party in India, founded in 1966 by Bal Thackeray. Originally formed to advocate for the rights of Marathi people in Maharashtra, it has evolved into a major political force in the state and has expanded its presence nationally.

Key Principles

  • Marathi Pride: Promoting Marathi language and culture
  • Hindutva: Hindu nationalist ideology
  • Regional Development: Focus on Maharashtra's growth
  • Employment: Jobs for local youth
  • Cultural Heritage: Preserving Maharashtra's traditions

Major Achievements

  • Governance in Mumbai Municipal Corporation
  • Infrastructure Development in Maharashtra
  • Promotion of Marathi Language and Culture
  • Employment Initiatives for Local Youth
  • Urban Development Projects
  • Support for Small Businesses and Traders

Vision for Future

Shiv Sena envisions a prosperous Maharashtra where Marathi culture thrives, local industries flourish, and the youth have ample employment opportunities. The party aims to make Maharashtra a leading state in India through development, cultural preservation, and good governance.
